Full Biography

In Her Own Words

I didn't grow up doubting God. I grew up asking questions no one seemed willing to sit with.

Before the arguments, before the theories, there was my grandmother — Mama Adúnní — telling stories in Igbajo, Osun State. Stories about courage, truth, and a God who was never afraid of being questioned.

Then came Zenithia. Brilliant. Logical. Certain it had outgrown faith.

There, I searched. I argued. I pressed every claim until it either bled or stood.

Christianity didn't survive because it felt comforting. It survived because it refused to collapse.

Now I speak to the next generation — the ones told faith is shallow and questions are dangerous. I've been where doubt lives. I know its language. And I've learned that truth does not fear examination.

This is not a brand. It's a journey still unfolding.

Walk with me.
