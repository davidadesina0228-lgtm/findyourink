The Hidden Logic of Zenithia: Designing a World of Truth

Every city is a philosophy. Zenithia is no different.

Vinyeze David-Adesina · February 5, 2025 · 5 min read

Architecture Reflecting Ancient Paths

Zenithia isn't just a fantasy setting — it's a physical representation of the intellectual and spiritual search. Every spire and street in the city is designed to ask a question. The towers that reach highest are the ones most likely to collapse. The markets that are loudest are the ones where the least real exchange happens.

"Every city is a philosophy. Zenithia is designed to be questioned."

The Logic Behind the World

When we built the logic of Zenithia, we asked a simple question: what would a world look like if it had successfully convinced itself that logic was the highest value, but had forgotten why logic mattered in the first place? The result is a city of extraordinary precision — and extraordinary emptiness.

It is beautiful and brittle. Advanced and lost. Exactly like every generation that has tried to outgrow the need for transcendence.

"The higher the tower, the louder the silence at the top."

What Abimifoluwa Finds There

Abimifoluwa doesn't arrive in Zenithia as a missionary. She arrives as a seeker. And what she finds is not a city that needs to be converted — but a city that has forgotten what it was searching for. Her journey is not to argue Zenithia into faith, but to help it remember its own questions.

This is the deepest apologetic there is: not proving God, but helping people rediscover their own longing for truth.
